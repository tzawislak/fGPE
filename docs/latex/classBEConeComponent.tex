\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item In constructor \doxylink{classBEConeComponent_afbda97922aa9c77560f53f6e1cc0c2e9}{BECone\+Component()}, you can define up to NO\+\_\+\+HAMIL\+\_\+\+PARAMS parameters your CUDA kernel Hamiltonian will have access to at runtime.
\item Initialize your wavefunction in \doxylink{classBEConeComponent_a676af718df659245ccc841fc950e4024}{Init\+Psi()}. You may write your own initialization as the class member function of \doxylink{classPsi}{Psi}.
\item Initialize your external potential in \doxylink{classBEConeComponent_a696f61899ad843d4a2e9346f6499019c}{Init\+Vext()}. You may write your own initialization as the class member function of \doxylink{classVext}{Vext}.
\item Write your CUDA kernel BECHamiltonian that will calculate the hamiltonian $\mathcal{H}|\psi\rangle$ and energ density functional $\mathcal{E}[\psi]$. The EDF is crucial in Imaginary time evolution and optional in real time evolution.
\item Write your CUDA kernel BECHamiltonian\+MU that will calculate the hamiltonian $\mathcal{H}|\psi\rangle$. This is used only in real time evolution and is needed alongside BECHamiltonian kernel for time efficiency \begin{DoxyWarning}{Warning}
Make sure your CUDA kernels BECHamiltonian and BECHamiltonian\+MU provide the same $\mathcal{H}|\psi\rangle$.
\end{DoxyWarning}

\item You may use the member functions call\+\_\+\+IM\texorpdfstring{$\ast$}{*} to log the imaginary time evolution (see descriptions of each function).
\item You may use the member functions call\+\_\+\+RE\texorpdfstring{$\ast$}{*} to log the real time evolution (see descriptions of each function). 
\end{DoxyEnumerate}
